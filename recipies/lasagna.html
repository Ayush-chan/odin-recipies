Lasagna Recipie

This lasagna is a towering achievement of Italian soul food, featuring layers of wide pasta ribbons nestled between a hearty, slow-simmered meat sauce and a creamy cheese blend. Unlike the lighter versions, this classic recipe focuses on a "low and slow" Bolognese made with ground beef and aromatics, which provides a deep, savory foundation. Each bite offers a perfect contrast between the slightly chewy pasta and the golden, bubbly crust of melted mozzarella.

What truly sets a great lasagna apart is the balance of moisture. By layering rich ricotta cheese—often brightened with fresh parsley and a hint of nutmeg—alongside plenty of parmesan, the dish achieves a luscious, velvety mouthfeel. As it bakes, the flavors marry together, creating a cohesive, sliceable masterpiece that is both rustic and sophisticated.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb Ground beef
  • 1 jar (24 oz) Marinara sauce
  • 12 Lasagna noodles
  • 15 oz Ricotta cheese
  • 3 cups Shredded Mozzarella
  • 1/2 cup Grated Parmesan
  • 1 Large egg (beaten)
  • 1 small Onion and 3 cloves Garlic
  • 1/4 cup Fresh parsley and 1 tsp Oregano

Steps

  1. Prepare the Meat Sauce: Brown the ground beef with diced onion and garlic. Drain fat, then stir in the marinara sauce and simmer on low for 15 minutes.
  2. Mix the Ricotta: In a medium bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, beaten egg, grated parmesan, and chopped parsley. Stir until well combined.
  3. Prepare Noodles: Boil noodles in salted water until al dente. Drain and lay them flat on parchment paper to prevent sticking.
  4. Layer the Dish: In a 9x13 inch baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce. Top with noodles, a layer of ricotta mixture, meat sauce, and mozzarella.
  5. Repeat: Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, ending with a generous layer of mozzarella and a sprinkle of dried oregano on top.
  6. Bake: Cover with foil and bake at 190°C (375°F) for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake for another 15–20 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and brown.
  7. Rest: Let the lasagna rest for at least 15 minutes before cutting to ensure clean, firm slices.

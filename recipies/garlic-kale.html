Garlic Kale Recipie

This dish transforms hardy, fibrous kale into a vibrant and tender side that bursts with savory flavor. By quickly sautéing the greens over medium-high heat, you preserve their deep emerald color while softening the texture just enough to make them pleasant to eat. The secret lies in the generous amount of thinly sliced garlic, which toasts in the olive oil to create a nutty, aromatic base that infuses every leaf.

To elevate the earthy notes of the kale, a final splash of fresh lemon juice is added right before serving. This acidity "brightens" the dish, cutting through the richness of the oil and the sulfurous edge of the greens. A pinch of red pepper flakes adds a subtle, warming heat, making this a versatile companion to any protein.

Ingredients

  • 2 large bunches Kale (stems removed)
  • 4–5 cloves Garlic (thinly sliced)
  • 2 tbsp Extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 fresh Lemon (for juice)
  • 1/4 tsp Red pepper flakes
  • 2 tbsp Water or vegetable broth
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Prep the Kale: Wash the kale thoroughly. Remove the tough woody stems. Tear or chop the leaves into bite-sized pieces and pat them dry.
  2. Infuse the Oil: Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced garlic and red pepper flakes. Sauté for about 1 minute until the garlic is fragrant and just starting to turn golden.
  3. Sauté the Greens: Increase the heat to medium-high and add the kale to the pan in handfuls. Toss frequently using tongs to coat the leaves in the garlic oil.
  4. Steam Soften: Add 2 tablespoons of water or broth to the pan. Cover with a lid for 1–2 minutes to help soften the kale.
  5. Season: Remove the lid and continue to sauté until the liquid has evaporated and the kale is tender but still bright green. Season with salt and black pepper.
  6. The Finish: Remove the pan from the heat. Squeeze the fresh lemon juice over the top and give it one final toss. Serve immediately.
